# Social Posts

Vee writes social posts from real data, not blank pages. Before drafting anything, pull trending topics, hook patterns, and posting cadence data from your Virlo niche monitor. The best-performing posts in any market share structural patterns - find them, then build from them.

---

## Before You Write Anything

Check Virlo first:

- **What topics are generating the most views in your niche this week?** Your niche monitor shows you. Start there.
- **What hook styles are dominating your market's top performers?** Pull from outlier analysis - look at the first line of their captions and the first text card of their videos.
- **What's your posting cadence data showing?** If your tracked creators are posting 5x per week and outperforming accounts posting 2x, that's a signal.
- **What did your own recent posts do?** The daily report shows views, likes, and engagement broken out. Build more of what's working.

## LinkedIn Posts

### What Makes a LinkedIn Post Work

LinkedIn's feed shows only the first 1-2 lines before "see more." Those lines are the entire post. If they don't earn the click, the rest is invisible.

The body rewards people who clicked. Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences maximum), white space between every thought, and either a clear argument or a story with a payoff.

The CTA asks for one action. Not three. Soft CTAs ("What's your experience?") warm the comments for visibility. Direct CTAs ("DM me [keyword]") generate leads.

### Post Types

**Thought leadership**
Takes a position. Makes a case. Doesn't hedge.

Structure:
- Line 1-2: The position, stated plainly
- Body: The argument, with a specific example or data point per paragraph
- CTA: Ask for their take or direct to a resource

Hook examples:
- "Most content strategies fail for the same reason."
- "The teams winning on short-form aren't posting more. They're researching differently."
- "[Specific niche] accounts that post from data consistently outperform those that post from instinct."

**Process reveal**
Shows how you do something specific. Practical, step-by-step, transferable.

Structure:
- Line 1-2: The result or the process name
- Body: The steps, one per paragraph, short
- CTA: Save this / try it / ask a question

Hook examples:
- "Here's how I build a content brief in under 10 minutes."
- "Every week I run through the same 4-step research process. Here it is."
- "The exact way I find what to post next."

**Perspective shift**
Changes how they see something they thought they already understood.

Structure:
- Line 1-2: The old view (stated as conventional wisdom)
- Pivot: "But..."
- The new frame, with evidence
- CTA: Soft - "What's your take?"

Hook examples:
- "Posting frequency matters less than most people think."
- "You don't need more content. You need better intelligence about what to create."
- "The data doesn't point where everyone says it does."

**Article promo**
Promotes a longer piece. Doesn't explain the insight - teases it.

Structure:
- Hook: Bold claim about the article's finding
- 1 data point or compelling detail
- "I broke down exactly:" + 3-4 bullet points listing what's inside (contents, not insights)
- Dual audience callout
- "Link in comments"

Key rules for article promo posts: don't give the insight away in the post. Arrow bullets show contents, not conclusions. One stat only - the viral number, nothing else. No follower count comparisons.

**Data insight**
Leads with a real number from your Virlo data. Explains what it means. Draws a conclusion.

Structure:
- Line 1: The number
- Lines 2-4: Context (what market, what timeframe, what it was compared against)
- Conclusion: What this tells you about what to do
- CTA: Engage or try it

Pull your data directly from your niche monitor results, outlier analysis, or tracked creator snapshots. Approximate to clean numbers (870K not 873,214).

**Personal story**
A specific thing that happened. What it revealed. What changed.

Structure:
- Line 1-2: The moment (specific, visual, time-stamped)
- What happened next
- What it revealed
- The takeaway
- CTA: Soft or none

Don't moralize at the end. Let the story make the point.

### Format Rules

- Proper sentence case throughout
- No walls of text - every paragraph is 1-2 sentences, then a line break
- No bullet points in the first 3 lines (they look like a list, not a hook)
- Numbers stand alone: write "14 million views" not "14M+ views"
- No em dashes
- Hashtags: 3-5 maximum, at the very end if used at all

### Character Limits

- Text: No hard limit, but posts over 1,300 characters see drop-off. Keep body under 800 words.
- Link previews: LinkedIn pulls them automatically. If you want a clean post, put the link in comments.

## X/Twitter Posts and Threads

### Single Posts

One sharp idea. Specificity beats cleverness. Numbers and contrasts drive the most engagement.

X posts live or die in 280 characters. Every word has to work.

**What works:**
- Specific numbers over vague claims: "One account in the supplement niche posted 9 videos last month - the one that hit 6 million views had the same hook structure as the top 3 accounts in the market."
- Contrarian takes: "Consistency is not the reason accounts blow up. It's the reason they don't die. The blow-up comes from one outlier."
- Data drops: real numbers from your Virlo niche monitor or tracked creators
- "Most people do X, top performers do Y" - use sparingly, be specific about what Y actually is
- Ranked lists: "3 hook types dominating [niche] right now: 1. 2. 3."

**What doesn't:**
- Vague advice without evidence
- Self-congratulatory announcements without proof
- Threads that could have been a single tweet

### Threads

A thread is only as good as its hook post. The hook post must stand completely alone - if someone reads only the first tweet and never expands, it should still deliver value.

Structure:
- Tweet 1 (hook): Standalone insight, data point, or provocative claim. Often ends with "Thread:" or is strong enough not to need it.
- Tweets 2-10 (body): One idea per tweet. Build on the previous. Don't repeat.
- Final tweet (CTA): Follow, repost, comment, or try something. One action.

Thread formats that perform:
- "X things I found when I analyzed [niche] with Virlo" - data-backed numbered list
- "The [specific topic] breakdown" - deep dive into one idea across multiple tweets
- "What [account/niche] is doing that nobody's talking about" - insider reveal
- "I was wrong about [thing]. Here's what the data actually shows." - perspective shift

### Voice Register

- Lowercase default (unless capitalizing for emphasis)
- Conversational, direct, no corporate language
- First person where possible
- Short sentences - Twitter readers skim faster than any other platform

### Engagement Patterns

Pull from your Virlo niche monitor to see what topics in your market are generating the most engagement on X. If you've connected your account through PostForMe, your analytics will show which of your own posts drove the most replies and reposts.

## Instagram Captions

### Structure

The first line is the hook. Instagram shows roughly the first 125 characters before "more." That line determines who reads the rest.

Body: Tell a story or deliver value. Emoji-light - 2-3 maximum, used for visual separation not decoration. Short paragraphs. Direct language.

CTA: One action. "Save this," "Share with someone who needs this," or a comment prompt ("What's your niche? Drop it below").

Hashtags: At the end, separated by a line break, or in the first comment. 5-10 relevant hashtags. Mix broad (#contentmarketing) with specific (#tiktokgrowth, #reelsstrategy).

### Caption Types

**Educational:** "Here's why [thing] works in [niche]." Body explains the mechanism. CTA: save.

**Behind-the-scenes:** "This is how I [process]." Body shows the steps. CTA: comment with questions.

**Data-driven:** Lead with a real number from your Virlo data. Context. Conclusion. CTA: share.

**Story:** Specific moment → what it revealed → takeaway. CTA: soft or none.

### Common Mistakes

- Front-loading hashtags (kills the hook)
- Captions that are longer than the value they deliver
- Emojis as filler instead of structure
- No CTA at all (wastes the engagement window)

## Reddit Posts

Reddit requires a different posture than any other platform. Communities are actively hostile to self-promotion and highly sensitive to inauthentic language. Value must come first, always.

### Before Posting

Read the subreddit for at least 10-15 posts before contributing. Every community has its own tone, rules, and tolerance for self-promotion. Posting without reading the room is the fastest way to get banned.

Check the subreddit rules sidebar. Most ban direct promotion. Some allow it in specific threads (weekly "share your project" posts, AMAs, etc.).

### Value-First Posts

The best Reddit strategy is to answer questions genuinely and completely, without mentioning your product unless it's directly relevant and the community allows it.

Structure for a value post:
- Direct answer to the question in the first paragraph
- Context or evidence (from your Virlo data if relevant - frame it as "I pulled data from my niche" not "I used X tool")
- Optional: subtle mention if the product is genuinely the right answer and the community allows it

### Lead Generation Approach

Find threads where your ICP is asking questions your product answers. Provide a complete, helpful answer. If direct promotion is allowed, a single sentence at the end: "I built something that automates this - happy to share if useful."

Never: cold product pitches in comment threads, posting the same comment across multiple subreddits, or asking for upvotes.

### Product Launch Community Seeding

If you're launching or announcing something, find communities where the announcement is genuinely relevant. Frame as "sharing something I built" not "promoting my product." Be in the comments responding to every reply. Upvote value. Provide more value than you take.

## Pinterest Descriptions

Pinterest is a search engine with a visual interface. Descriptions must be optimized for search, not just for reading.

### Structure

- First sentence: the primary keyword phrase, naturally integrated
- Body: additional context, benefit, or how-to detail
- CTA: "Save for [specific use case]" or "Try this for [outcome]"

### SEO Guidance

Use keywords people actually search: "content research tools," "TikTok growth strategy," "how to find trending content," "short-form video ideas." Pull keyword ideas from your niche monitor - the topics your niche is posting about are the topics people are searching for.

Keep descriptions between 100-500 characters. Front-load the keywords. Avoid generic filler ("check this out!").

### What Works on Pinterest

- Specific outcomes: "How to find 30 content ideas in one hour"
- Step-by-step promises: "The 6-step process for building a content calendar from data"
- Saved reference content: checklists, frameworks, step-by-step guides

Pinterest engagement is almost entirely saves, not comments. Write descriptions that make someone think "I want to come back to this."

## SPARKS Diagnostic for Social Posts

Every post hits some SPARKS layers and skips others by design. Know which you're hitting:

- **S (Sting):** Does the hook make the reader feel a problem or a missed opportunity?
- **P (Prize):** Is it clear what the reader gets from engaging with this post?
- **A (Anchor):** Is there a real data point, result, or specific example grounding the claim?
- **R (Riddle):** Does the hook create a gap the reader needs to close?
- **K (Knockout):** Does the post address the most obvious reason someone would dismiss it?
- **S (Seal):** Is the CTA framed as a natural next step?

A LinkedIn thought leadership post might run S-P-A. A data drop tweet might run A-R. A thread might hit all six across its arc. Match the layers to the format and the goal.
