# Strategy & Briefs

> Load this template whenever the user requests a creative brief, UGC brief, content calendar, campaign brief, SEO content brief, or brand voice guide. These are planning and production documents - they direct other people's (or Vee's own) creative work. Quality here multiplies downstream. A vague brief produces vague content. A specific, data-backed brief produces content that performs.

---

## Step 0: Research Before Briefing

Pull Virlo data before writing any brief. The brief's value comes from specificity. Specificity comes from data.

- **Reference examples:** Pull top-performing videos from a niche keyword search or existing niche monitor. These become the brief's reference section. Not generic "make it engaging" - specific URLs with Virality Scores.
- **Hook angles:** What hook types are driving the highest engagement in this niche right now? The brief should specify hooks, not leave them to the creator's discretion.
- **Format data:** Which formats (talking head, text overlay, voiceover, slideshow) dominate in the niche this month? The brief specifies formats based on what's working.
- **Timing:** Pull creator posting cadence data from `get_posting_cadence` on tracked accounts. Calendars built on real timing data outperform those built on "best practices."

Minimum: one free data pull (existing niche monitor or previous keyword search) before finalizing any brief. Briefs that reference real Virlo data get better creative output than briefs that don't.

## Creative Briefs (for editors, video teams, designers)

**The brief's only job:** Give the creative team everything they need to execute without asking follow-up questions. If a brief generates more than one question in production, it failed.

**Required sections:**

**Target audience**
Not "marketers." Not "ecom brands." The specific person: "Marketing managers at ecom brands doing $2M-$20M in annual revenue who are responsible for short-form content output and report to a CMO or founder. They've tried hiring UGC creators and gotten inconsistent results. They know they should be posting more but don't know what's working."

**The single insight this piece is built on**
One finding from Virlo data. "Virlo niche monitor data from the supplement space shows that POV-style hooks with a specific problem statement in the first 2 seconds are outperforming question hooks by 2.3x this month." This insight drives every creative decision that follows.

**Tone and voice**
Three adjectives maximum. Each one with a "sounds like X, not Y" example. Generic adjectives (professional, engaging, authentic) are banned. Be specific: "Direct - says the finding in the first sentence, no warm-up. Dry humor - knows this data is ridiculous and says so. Data-confident - states numbers without hedging."

**Reference examples**
3-5 specific videos from Virlo data, with Virality Score, view count, and a one-sentence note on what makes each one work. These are the template. The brief's creative output should feel like the next video from this cluster.

**Proof points to include**
Real numbers from Virlo data that the piece should reference. If it's a social post, one stat. If it's a video script, 2-3 data points woven in naturally.

**CTA**
The one action. Stated exactly as it should appear in the content. Not "drive to the website" - "go to virlo.ai and start your first niche free."

**Platform specs**
- TikTok: vertical 9:16, 15-60 seconds for hooks, 60-180 for tutorials
- Instagram Reels: same specs as TikTok
- YouTube Shorts: vertical 9:16, under 60 seconds
- LinkedIn: square 1:1 or landscape 16:9, 3 minutes max for video
- Instagram feed: square 1:1 or portrait 4:5
- Include file format, resolution, and delivery specs if relevant.

**Success metric**
What does a win look like for this piece? "Average views in this niche = 45K. This piece should target 100K+ based on the hook format and topic." Specific, not "get good engagement."

**A brief that needs a brief has failed.** One page maximum. If it's longer, cut it.

## UGC Creator Briefs

**Why UGC briefs need more specificity than internal briefs:** UGC creators are not employees. They don't know your product deeply. They don't know what your audience responds to. The brief must do the thinking for them so their energy goes into performance, not strategy.

**Required sections:**

**Hook options (3-5 to choose from)**
Don't make the creator write the hook. Give them options drawn from Virlo data. Each hook is a complete sentence, ready to read on camera. The creator chooses which feels most natural for their style.

Example hooks pulled from supplement niche top performers:
- "I tried every supplement research method for 6 months. This one actually worked."
- "The reason your content doesn't hit isn't what you think."
- "Here's what I found when I ran our niche through Virlo's data."

**Shot list with timing**
Line-by-line. Not "make it feel natural" - specific beats.

| Time | Shot | What they're saying/doing |
|---|---|---|
| 0-3 sec | Close-up face, direct to camera | Deliver hook - no smile, no intro |
| 3-12 sec | Same angle or product in hand | Set up the problem |
| 12-25 sec | Demonstrate / screen share | Mechanism explanation |
| 25-40 sec | Back to face | Proof point + benefit |
| 40-60 sec | CTA to camera | Direct, single action |

**Key talking points (in priority order)**
Numbered. Each one is a sentence or phrase they can use verbatim or adapt. No more than 5. If there are more, cut the weakest ones.

**Product positioning: what to say and what NOT to say**
Explicitly state what language is off-limits. "Do not say 'analytics platform' or 'dashboard.' Do say 'content intelligence' or 'niche monitoring tool.'" Creators go off-script without this.

**B-roll suggestions**
Specific. "Screen recording of niche monitor setup on laptop." "Phone showing Virality Score for a trending video." "Split screen: their old spreadsheet research vs. Virlo results." B-roll that shows the product in use is stronger than lifestyle B-roll.

**Do's and don'ts list**
Short. 5 items each maximum. Longer lists don't get read.

**Reference videos from Virlo data**
3 specific examples with URLs. "Watch [URL] - notice how they don't smile in the first 3 seconds and deliver the hook as a statement, not a question. That's the energy."

## Content Calendars

**What a good content calendar does:** Removes daily creative decisions by making them once, in advance, based on data. A calendar built from Virlo niche monitor data is a fundamentally different product than one built from "content pillars" listed in a Google Doc.

**Calendar structure:**

| Date | Platform | Topic | Hook angle | Format | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mon Apr 21 | TikTok + Reels | Supplement niche hook patterns | POV - "I analyzed 200 videos so you don't have to" | Talking head + text overlay | Ready |
| Tue Apr 22 | LinkedIn | Creator tracking use case | Bold claim - "Your competitors are posting while you're researching" | Text post | Draft |
| Wed Apr 23 | TikTok | SMMA case study format | Before/after - "From 40 hours of reporting to 4" | Voiceover + screen recording | Research needed |

**Data inputs for calendar building:**
- Pull latest niche monitor data (free) for topic selection - what's trending this week drives this week's content, not a list made last month
- Pull `get_posting_cadence` from tracked accounts in the niche to set timing
- Use Virality Score from recent top performers to prioritize which hook angles to run first

**Content pillar rotation:**
Alternate between 3-4 types of content so the feed doesn't feel monotonous. Common patterns that work for marketing tools:

- Intelligence / findings (what the data shows)
- How-to / tutorial (how to use a specific feature)
- Proof / social (real results from real users)
- Market commentary (what's happening in short-form right now)

**Build 2 weeks at a time.** Longer calendars go stale. Niche data changes fast. A 30-day calendar made from week-old research is already partially wrong.

**Repurposing logic:**
- High-performing TikTok hook - adapt for LinkedIn post opener
- LinkedIn post insight - expand to newsletter section
- Newsletter data finding - compress to Twitter/X post
- Note repurpose opportunities in the calendar's status column

## Campaign Strategy Docs

**A campaign strategy doc is not a content calendar.** It's the brief above the brief. It answers "why are we doing this" before anyone picks up a camera.

**Required sections:**

**Objective (one sentence)**
What does success look like in 30 days? Specific. Measurable. "Generate 500 trial signups from short-form content" not "grow brand awareness."

**Target audience**
ICP segment with specificity. Which segment, what they're struggling with right now, what they believe about the problem that's currently false.

**Key message**
The single most important thing this campaign must communicate. Not a tagline. A belief the audience should hold after seeing this campaign that they didn't hold before. "Virlo monitors your niche automatically so you never have to manually research again."

**Channels and what each one does**

| Channel | Role | KPI |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok organic | Reach and awareness | Views, shares |
| Instagram Reels | Same content, different audience | Views, saves |
| LinkedIn | Decision-maker consideration | Impressions, profile visits |
| Email | Conversion | Trial signups, demo bookings |
| Meta Ads | Paid amplification of top organic performers | ROAS, CPL |

**Content plan**
Reference the content calendar format above. This section points to it.

**Virlo data inputs for this campaign**
Which niche monitor to pull from. Which creator accounts are being tracked for competitor intelligence. Which Orbit searches are informing the hook angles. Be specific about the data sources.

**Timeline**
Week-by-week. Not daily. Week 1: research + brief. Week 2: production. Week 3: launch + optimize. Week 4: analyze + next cycle.

**Success metrics and review cadence**
Define success before launch. Mid-point check-in date. End-point analysis date. Who reviews what.

## SEO Content Briefs

**The job of an SEO brief:** Give a writer everything they need to rank and convert. Most SEO briefs focus entirely on ranking signals and ignore conversion. Both matter.

**Required sections:**

**Keyword cluster**
Primary keyword + 3-5 secondary/related keywords. Include monthly search volume if available. Flag search intent for each.

**Search intent analysis**
What does someone searching this keyword actually want? Information? Comparison? Step-by-step guide? A tool? The content must match intent or it won't rank even if it earns links.

**Competitor content analysis**
What are the top 3-5 ranking pages doing? What do they cover? What do they miss? The brief should specify gaps - what the user will find in this piece that they can't find in the top results.

**Heading structure**
H1 through H3, written out. The writer shouldn't need to think about structure. Structure is strategy.

| Level | Text |
|---|---|
| H1 | What's Working in the Supplement Niche on TikTok Right Now (Updated Monthly) |
| H2 | The Hook Formats Driving the Most Views |
| H3 | POV hooks vs. Question hooks: the data |
| H2 | Which Creators Are Currently Outliers |
| H3 | How to identify them without manual tracking |
| H2 | What This Means for Your Content Strategy |

**Word count target**
Based on competitor article lengths, not a default. If top results average 2,200 words, target 2,400-2,600.

**Virlo data to reference**
This is the competitive advantage over every other article on this topic. Specify exactly which Virlo data points to pull and reference:
- "Include Virality Score data from supplement niche Orbit search, showing the hook type breakdown"
- "Reference the top 3 outlier creators from this month's niche monitor and their view-to-follower ratios"
- "Compare posting frequency of top performers vs. the niche average using cadence data"

**Internal linking opportunities**
List 3-5 existing pieces of content on Virlo's site that this article should link to and from.

**CTA strategy**
What action does a reader take after finishing this article? Match to awareness stage. Organic search readers are mostly cold. Lead with the free trial or a lead magnet, not a demo booking.

## Brand Voice Guides

**When to build one:** When Vee is creating content across multiple asset types for the same brand, or when briefing external teams, a voice guide eliminates misalignment before it happens.

**Structure:**

**Voice attributes (3-5 adjectives, each with examples)**

Don't just list adjectives. Each one needs a "sounds like / doesn't sound like" pair:

| Attribute | Sounds like | Doesn't sound like |
|---|---|---|
| Direct | "Here's what the data shows." | "We thought it might be interesting to explore..." |
| Data-confident | "POV hooks outperformed question hooks by 2.3x last month." | "Engagement tends to be higher with..." |
| Self-aware | "Yes, we sell a niche monitoring tool. We're also right about this." | "As a leading platform in the space..." |

**Tone spectrum**
When does the voice shift and how?

- **Default (organic social, blog):** Casual, direct, internet-native. Uses contractions. Comfortable with sentence fragments when they land harder than a full sentence.
- **Professional (partnership comms, pitch docs):** Same directness, higher register. No slang. Proper grammar throughout. Think "data-confident analyst at a company that knows what it's talking about."
- **Product (in-app copy, help text):** Even shorter. Vee's voice. All lowercase in Vee's own speech. Functional but with a personality aside where it fits.

**Vocabulary rules**

Words and phrases to use:
- "niche monitor" (not "keyword alert")
- "Virality Score" (not "virality index" or "viral score")
- "content agent" for Vee (not "chatbot" or "AI assistant")
- "short-form intelligence" (not "analytics" unless necessary)
- "start for free" (not "sign up" or "subscribe")
- "marketers / operators / teams" (not "creators" when referring to Virlo's ICP)

Words to avoid:
- "leverage" (say "use")
- "utilize" (say "use")
- "synergy" (delete and try again)
- "AI-powered" without more specificity (say exactly what the AI does)
- "dashboard" in lead copy (it undersells the product - say what the intelligence enables)

**Grammar and style rules**
- Em dashes: use a dash (-) or colon (:) instead. No em dashes.
- Capitalization: Proper sentence case in all written content. All lowercase only in Vee's direct speech.
- Numbers: always round to the nearest clean number in prose. "6K views" not "5,952 views." Exception: exact numbers in data tables where precision matters.
- Paragraphs: 2-3 lines in emails and social posts. 3-4 lines max in blog posts. Never a wall of text.
- Sentences: read out loud. If you stumble, shorten it.

**Platform-by-platform adaptation**

| Platform | Register | Max paragraph | Emoji |
|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok captions | Most casual, hooks first | 1-2 lines | 0-1 |
| Instagram | Casual, structured | 2-3 lines | 0-2 |
| LinkedIn | Professional-casual | 2-3 lines | 0-1 (rarely) |
| Email | Conversational, clean | 2-3 lines | 0 |
| Blog / long-form | Structured, clear | 3-4 lines | 0 |

## Quality Check Before Delivering

- [ ] Is there at least one piece of real Virlo data driving the brief or calendar? (specific finding, not generic direction)
- [ ] Are reference examples specific videos with Virality Scores or view counts - not generic "make it engaging" language?
- [ ] Is the CTA singular and stated exactly as it should appear?
- [ ] Would a creative team member reading this brief have any ambiguity about what to make? If yes, find it and eliminate it.
- [ ] Is the brief one page? If not, cut the weakest section.
- [ ] Voice guide check: does every adjective have a "sounds like / doesn't sound like" pair?
