# Short-Form Video Content

Vee creates short-form video content grounded in what's actually performing in your niche right now. The best scripts aren't invented - they're reverse-engineered from outliers.

---

## Before You Write Anything

**Step 1: Pull Virlo data.**

- **Trending hooks:** What hooks appear in your niche's top-performing videos this week? Check your niche monitor and outlier results.
- **Format patterns:** Are carousels outperforming talking-head right now? Is a specific frame (tutorial, reaction, storytime) dominating views?
- **Posting cadence:** When are your tracked creators posting? What day and time are their top videos going up?
- **Virality signals:** Which videos in your niche have the highest views-to-followers ratio? Those are your source templates.

**Step 2: Pick ONE specific source video.** From the outlier data, select the single video that best matches what the user wants to create. Extract its full transcript - every word spoken, every text overlay, every visual transition.

**Step 3: Break it into beats.** Identify the structural sections of the source video. A story video has different beats than a listicle. A tutorial has different beats than a hot take. Don't force a fixed template - read the source and identify its actual structure: where does the hook end? Where does the body shift topics? Where does proof appear? Where does the CTA land? How many distinct sections exist?

**Step 4: Build the beat-map table.** Two columns: Original Beat (the source video's actual content at each beat) | Your Beat (the user's version, using their brand voice, their product, their audience). Show this table before writing the script.

**Step 5: Write the script from the table.** The table IS the draft. Convert the right column into the final script format. The structure is already proven - you're changing the nouns, not the architecture.

The script you write should feel like it belongs in the feed alongside the top 10 performers in your niche - because it was built from deconstructing them.

## TikTok / Reels / Shorts Scripts

### Structure

Every short-form video script has three jobs. The hook stops the scroll. The body delivers the idea. The CTA captures the response.

**Hook (0-2 seconds)**
The first words and first frame decide everything. If the hook fails, nobody sees the rest.

Hook types:
- **Question:** "Why does [X] outperform [Y] every single time?"
- **Bold claim:** "This is why your content isn't growing."
- **Shock stat:** "One video in this niche hit 14 million views in seven days."
- **Direct address:** "If you're a [specific type of person], stop scrolling."
- **Story tease:** "I almost quit doing this until I noticed something weird in the data."
- **Controversy:** "Everyone says post every day. They're wrong."
- **Tutorial promise:** "Here's exactly how to find trending content before anyone else."
- **Before/after:** "Six months ago I had no idea what to post. Now every video I make is based on data."

Pull the hook type that's dominating in your niche this week from Virlo's outlier analysis. If question hooks are outperforming everything else in your market, start there.

**Body (3-30 seconds)**
One idea. Delivered clearly. No tangents.

The body's job is to deliver the value promised in the hook. If the hook teased a stat, explain it. If it promised a tutorial, do the tutorial. Don't pivot to a different topic.

Keep it tight. Short-form audiences exit the moment the video stops rewarding their attention.

**CTA (last 2-3 seconds)**
One action. Not three options.

- Follow: "Follow for more content intelligence"
- Comment: "Comment your niche below"
- Save: "Save this for when you're planning your next post"
- Click: "Link in bio to try it free"

Choose based on your goal. Building an audience: follow or comment. Building a content library: save. Driving trial: click.

### Timing Reference

- 15-second video: Hook 0-2s, Body 3-12s, CTA 13-15s
- 30-second video: Hook 0-3s, Body 4-25s, CTA 26-30s
- 60-second video: Hook 0-3s, Body 4-52s, CTA 53-60s

Longer does not mean more valuable. Go as short as the idea allows.

## Slideshow Carousels (6-Slide Formula)

Carousels trade reach for saves. They perform best when the information is genuinely worth returning to. Pull your carousel topics from your niche monitor - look for educational content that's generating high engagement in your market.

### The 6 Slides

**Slide 1: Hook slide**
This is your headline. Most people see only this slide before deciding whether to swipe. Treat it like a cover.

- Bold, large text. One sentence maximum.
- Pattern interrupt visual: high contrast, unexpected image, or stark simplicity.
- The text should promise something specific. "The 3 content formats dominating [niche] right now" beats "content tips."

Image gen prompt guidance: High contrast background (deep navy, black, or brand color). Large white text overlay. No clutter. No stock photo aesthetics. Clean, editorial.

**Slide 2: Problem/pain**
Name the specific, relatable problem your audience has. The more precise, the better.

Use a real pain from your niche research. If your Virlo data shows a specific topic cluster dominating in your market, the pain is probably the "before" state of that topic.

Text overlay: 2-3 short sentences. No walls of text. One idea per line.

**Slide 3: Mechanism/insight**
The "new" thing. The reframe. The thing they didn't know.

This is where curiosity does its job - check the SPARKS framework (R: Riddle Me This) for how to frame a mechanism without giving everything away. Name it in a way that makes them want to know more.

Image gen prompt guidance: Diagram-style visual or conceptual illustration. Something that visually represents "a system" or "a new way."

**Slide 4: Proof/example**
Back up the mechanism with a real data point, result, or example.

Pull from your Virlo niche monitor or tracked creator data. "One account in [niche] using this format averaged 870K views per video over 30 days" is proof. "This works really well" is not.

Image gen prompt guidance: Screenshot-style mockup, stat callout, or before/after comparison. Data visualization if relevant.

**Slide 5: How-to/steps**
Make it actionable. What do they do with this information?

Numbered steps. Short sentences. Concrete actions. If you've set up a niche monitor in Virlo, reference what data they should be looking at and what to do with it.

**Slide 6: CTA slide**
One clear next step.

- "Save this for your next content planning session"
- "Follow for weekly intelligence from your market"
- "Comment your niche below - I'll tell you what's working"
- "Try Virlo free at virlo.ai/?via=organic"

Image gen prompt guidance: Brand-consistent. Contact or handle visible. Clean and memorable.

### Text Overlay Guidance

- Font size: Large enough to read on mobile without zooming. If it looks big on desktop, it's probably right for mobile.
- Placement: Top third or center. Never in the bottom 20% where UI elements cover it.
- Contrast: White text on dark background or dark text on light background. No exceptions for readability.
- Line length: 5-7 words per line maximum. Break it earlier than you think.

## Hook Variations

For any concept, generate 10-15 hook variations before choosing one. You're looking for the version that stops the most scrolls for your specific audience.

### How to Generate Variations

Start with the core idea. Then write one hook in each of these styles:

1. Question
2. Bold claim
3. Shock stat (pull real numbers from Virlo)
4. Story tease
5. Direct address / identity callout
6. Controversy / contrarian take
7. Tutorial promise
8. Before/after
9. "I used to think..."
10. Numbered list tease ("3 things I found in my niche data...")
11. Failure → lesson frame
12. Warning ("Stop doing X until you read this")
13. Social proof tease ("Why [niche]'s top accounts are posting this format")
14. Curiosity gap ("What I found when I analyzed 500 videos in [niche]...")
15. Negative framing ("The worst-performing content in [niche] all has one thing in common")

### A/B Testing Guidance

If you can only test one thing per video, test the hook.

Use your Virlo tracked video data to compare view counts across hook types over time. After 10+ videos, patterns will emerge. Double down on the hook style that's generating the highest view-to-follower ratio for your account.

Post the same concept with two different hooks on consecutive days. Different platforms, or same platform at different times. Track which drives higher watch time and completion rate.

## UGC-Style Scripts

UGC content performs best when it feels personal and unscripted, even when it isn't. Pull your format inspiration from your Virlo niche monitor - look at which accounts in your market are generating the highest engagement, not just views. High engagement on a UGC-style video signals the format is resonating.

### Problem-Solution Format

Structure: Name the problem (in first person) → Explain what they tried → Introduce the solution → Show the result

```
HOOK: "I was spending [X hours] every week doing [painful task]. Then I found this."
BODY: "I used to [old method]. It was [specific pain]. Then I started [new approach] and..."
RESULT: "[Specific outcome]"
CTA: "If you want to try it, [one action]"
```

Shot list: Talking head for problem/solution. B-roll of the painful process. Screen recording or product demo for the solution. Close-up reaction shot for the result.

### Testimonial Format

Structure: Context → Specific result → How → CTA

```
HOOK: "[Your name/role] just [specific result] using [mechanism]."
CONTEXT: "I've been [doing thing] for [timeframe]. I never expected to..."
RESULT: "[Specific, measurable outcome]"
HOW: "The difference was [mechanism explained simply]"
CTA: "[One action]"
```

Shot list: On-camera testimony. Optional: screenshot of result, screen recording, or reaction shot.

### Demo/Walkthrough Format

Structure: Promise → Show → Explain → CTA

Best for product features. Pull from your niche monitor to identify which features or capabilities your audience is actively searching for.

```
HOOK: "Here's how I [specific task] in under [timeframe]."
DEMO: [Screen recording or physical demonstration, narrated in real time]
EXPLAIN: "What's happening is [simple explanation]"
CTA: "[One action]"
```

Shot list: Screen recording with voiceover. Optional talking head intro/outro. Text overlays labeling what's happening.

### Before/After Format

Structure: Before state → The change → After state → CTA

```
HOOK: "Before [mechanism]: [painful reality]. After: [desirable reality]."
BEFORE: "[Specific, visual description of the before state]"
CHANGE: "The moment everything changed was [turning point]"
AFTER: "[Specific, visual description of the after state]"
CTA: "[One action]"
```

Shot list: Two clearly contrasting setups. Physical, visual before-and-after where possible. Reaction shots.

### Unboxing/First Impression Format

Structure: Anticipation → First contact → Reaction → Verdict → CTA

Use when launching a new feature, sharing a new tool, or reacting to niche data for the first time.

```
HOOK: "I just got access to [new thing]. Here's my first reaction."
ANTICIPATION: "I've been waiting for this because [specific reason]."
FIRST CONTACT: [Real-time reaction, narrated]
VERDICT: "The thing that surprised me most was [genuine observation]."
CTA: "[One action]"
```

## Trending Format Adaptations

### How to Identify Trending Formats from Virlo

Open your niche monitor results and look for format clusters:

- If 3+ top-performing videos in the last 7 days use the same visual style (talking head, text-only, green screen, duet, B-roll montage), that format is winning right now.
- Look at your outlier data: if videos with views-to-followers ratios well above average share a format type, that's your signal.
- Check posting cadence data on your tracked creators. When do their highest-performing videos go up? What format did they use?

### How to Adapt a Trending Format to Your Niche

1. Identify what makes the format work (the hook structure, the visual style, the pacing, the audio choice).
2. Separate the format from the specific topic. "Tutorial with rapid cuts and text callouts" is the format. "How to lose weight" is the topic.
3. Apply the format to your niche's topic. Keep the structure. Change the content.
4. Don't copy the exact script. Adapt the pattern.

### When to Ride a Trend vs. When It's Too Late

**Ride it:** The format appeared in your niche's top performers in the last 7 days and is still appearing in new uploads. Volume is growing.

**Pass on it:** The format has been in your niche for 3+ weeks and the view counts on new uploads using it are declining. It peaked.

**Never chase:** Audio trends, dances, or memes from outside your niche that don't connect naturally to your content. Forced adaptation reads as desperate.

Use your Virlo niche monitor to track format lifespan. If you've been monitoring a niche for 30+ days, you'll see patterns emerge and die. That's your timing guide.

## SPARKS Diagnostic for Short-Form Video

Run any script through the relevant SPARKS layers before shooting:

- **S (Sting):** Does the hook make the viewer feel the problem or the missed opportunity within 2 seconds?
- **P (Prize):** Is the outcome of watching this video clear within the first 5 seconds?
- **A (Anchor):** Is there any proof in the video - a real number, a real result, a real example?
- **R (Riddle):** Does the hook create a gap the viewer needs to close by watching the whole video?
- **K (Knockout):** Does the script anticipate the most common reason someone would stop watching and address it?
- **S (Seal):** Is the CTA framed as a natural next step, not a demand?

Short-form video rarely hits all six layers. A 15-second video might only hit S and R. That's fine. Know which layers you're hitting and make sure they're strong.
